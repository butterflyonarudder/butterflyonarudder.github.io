So I'm starting this blog thing. Things are taking an incredible shape in my life and part of my new vocabulary has been the phrase Open Source.

I have understood this to live mostly in design ideas in a very linear- computer program or physical invention- type setting. My fella gave me a new meaning this morning. We are in constant dialog about supporting ourselves sustainably, during part of that dialog this morning, he asked me to open source my life to the Universe, or at least that part of the universe reachable by internet.

"Eileen, please write."

Um. Dear lad, you know not what you ask. "Find me time," I said. I went to a programmers' meeting with him, crawled into my headphones and began.

In the Beginning, I became a single mom with three damaged, middle-sized boys. I worked part time at a fabrics and crafts store, learned to teach knitting and crocheting, sewing, playing with papaer and beads and wire, and finally, cake decorating. I had a group of fabulous women who stayed with me for 3 months. They allowed me to learn the material for the second and third courses as I was teaching them. I am indebted. Especially to one who was in a similar family situation and the same age, within months of me, actually. She was also in the Nursing Program at our community college. Several months before, I had realized that I was practicing (habitually) as a mental health therapist at just above minimum wage. Now I realized that School was an option. In order to do what I wanted, get paid an equitable wage for using the gifts that I enjoy using; to obtain a license as a mental health counselor, I needed a Master's degree in clinical psychology.

I'm four semesters in. I love it. My life has continued to shift as I continue to awaken.

Today I began a project for a local hotel, hemming half their draperies to accomodate new ac units. I'm charging $20/hour. This opportunity came from a friend of a friend.

Today I also obtained certification for my yard as Wildlife Habitat.

I have Oyster Mushrooms growing both in my yard and in containers in my house. I save my coffee grounds and stuff them back in their bags, after inoculating them with spawn, poke holes in the bag, put it in a plastic salad box (the kind that salad comes in at the grocers, or a plastic cake box from the grocery bakery). In the yard, the grounds are rolled into a pizza box, like a big jelly roll. I have also slurry-planted Shaggy Mane and Shaggy Parasol that I've gathered locally, and some Agaricus variety (given to my fella by a local Mushroom expert) in compost and stick piles around my yard. I worked my tail feathers off last Summer for some guys with a tree nursery and earned some serious trees. Sweet Cherry, Liberty Apple, Comice Pear, Seckle Pear, 3 yellow Plums; these will all have fruit next season. I also got some younger nut trees; Hazels and a Chinese Chestnut, a bunch of Currant shoots, a dozen or so juvenile Grape vines, as well as a couple of Blackberries, handful of BlackCap Raspberries, Chokecherry and native Plum shoots, a Saskatoon, Strawberries, a pair of Highbush Cranberries, and a pair of Sea Buckthorns. (I warned my landlord when I moved in that I have a habit of planting things, he said, 'go for it.')

I also make things for sale, but don't have the patience for craft fairs or farmer's markets. I set up a website, but then let the fee for the builder I used slide and now my page says something like, "under construction". Would like to get that up and running again.

Oh, yes. I'll be 43 this Summer.

I love you, Bridget the Writing Pixie!
